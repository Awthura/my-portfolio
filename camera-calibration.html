Camera Network Calibration for Digital Twin Systems

Production Camera Calibration | Computer Vision | Real-Time Localization | Sentics GmbH

Current Role: AI/ML/Deployment/Software Engineer at Sentics GmbH

Project Overview

At Sentics GmbH, I calibrate large-scale camera networks at customer production sites, handling deployments ranging from 10 to 160 cameras. Using a novel point-clicking calibration method developed by a colleague, I perform camera extrinsics calibration that forms the foundation for the entire real-time localization and mapping system. I also contribute to the ongoing development of the calibration tool, making it scalable for enterprise deployments.

Key Responsibilities

  • Camera extrinsics calibration at customer production sites (10-160 cameras)
  • Precision calibration using novel point-clicking methodology
  • Contributing to calibration tool development and scalability improvements
  • Ensuring calibration accuracy for real-time localization systems
  • On-site deployment and validation of calibration parameters

Technology Stack

Camera Calibration Computer Vision Camera Extrinsics 3D Geometry Real-Time Systems Production Deployment

Technical Highlights

  • Large-scale deployments: Successfully calibrated networks with up to 160 cameras
  • Novel methodology: Point-clicking approach for efficient extrinsics calibration
  • Critical dependency: Calibration quality directly impacts localization accuracy
  • Tool development: Contributing to scalability and workflow improvements
  • Production environment: Working with live customer systems under time constraints

Impact

Accurate camera calibration is fundamental to the digital twin system's ability to perform real-time localization and mapping. My calibration work ensures that spatial intelligence applications can accurately track objects and people across large areas, enabling critical business functions like occupancy monitoring, safety compliance, and workflow optimization for enterprise clients.
